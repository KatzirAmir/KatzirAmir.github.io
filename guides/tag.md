# What is the UC TAG Agreement?

**TAG** means **Transfer Admission Guarantee**. It is a program where participating **University of California** campuses offer a **guaranteed admission pathway** to eligible California community college students who complete a specific agreement.

Nothing feels better than a backstop. TAG is that backstop for many students - while they still apply more broadly to selective campuses.

---

## The core idea

1. You select **one** TAG campus (rules: one TAG). 
2. You submit a TAG application through the **UC Transfer Admission Planner (TAP)** by the deadline. 
3. You complete the required courses and GPA in the agreement. 
4. You still file the regular **UC application** in the normal window. 
5. If you honor the TAG, that campus’s guarantee applies (major/campus conditions matter).

Official overview: [UC TAG page](https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/transfer-requirements/uc-transfer-programs/transfer-admission-guarantee-tag.html).

---

## Which campuses participate?

Historically, **six** UC campuses participate in TAG. **UC Berkeley, UCLA, and UC San Diego do not offer TAG** in the usual program structure - those remain competitive review campuses.

Always download the current **TAG matrix** from UC Admissions for:

- Participating campuses 
- GPA minimums by campus 
- Major exceptions (some majors are closed to TAG) 
- Unit timing rules 

---

## High-level eligibility themes

Exact numbers change by campus and year. Conceptually you must:

- Be enrolled at a California community college (with residency/attendance patterns TAG requires) 
- Complete a minimum number of UC-transferable units by set terms 
- Finish required English and math courses on the TAG timeline 
- Maintain the **TAG GPA** for your campus (often higher for some campuses than the UC minimum) 
- Complete major prep if the TAG for that major requires it 
- Apply to UC for the correct term 

If you miss a condition after approval, you can lose the guarantee.

## Deadlines (verify each cycle)

UC’s published pattern (confirm current year):

| Step | Typical timing (fall transfer) |
|------|--------------------------------|
| Submit TAG via TAP | By **September 30** |
| Submit UC application | **October 1 – December 1** (fall) |
| Maintain grades / complete courses | Through the agreement’s end date |
| TAU / updates | As UC directs after apply |

There are also winter/spring TAG timelines for campuses that offer those terms (less common for some students).

---

## TAG vs “I want Berkeley”

Use TAG as **insurance**, not as your only ambition.

Example strategy pattern:

- TAG to a strong campus where your major is open and GPA is attainable 
- Also apply to non-TAG selective campuses (Berkeley, UCLA, etc.) if competitive 
- Keep major prep aligned so non-TAG apps stay strong 

Never sabotage Berkeley-level prep just to chase a easy TAG major you will hate - but do be honest about admit rates.

---

## TAP (Transfer Admission Planner)

[UC TAP](https://uctap.universityofcalifornia.edu/students/) is where you:

- Enter coursework 
- Build the TAG application 
- Track progress 

Treat TAP like a living spreadsheet. Update after every term. Errors in course entry cause painful delays.

---

## Common TAG failure modes

1. Wrong math/English timing 
2. GPA calculated on the wrong set of courses 
3. Choosing a major excluded from TAG 
4. Submitting TAG but forgetting the UC application 
5. Dropping a required course after TAG approval 
6. Unit residency issues (not enough CCC units from qualifying colleges)

---

## How dual enrollment interacts with TAG

High school dual enrollment can help you reach unit and course milestones earlier - which is how some students become TAG-ready soon after leaving high school. You still need to satisfy **TAG’s enrollment and unit pattern rules** as a CCC student, so map this with a counselor early.
