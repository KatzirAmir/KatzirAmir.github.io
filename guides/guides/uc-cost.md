# Cost of the University of California

UC publishes **estimated average costs** that include tuition, campus fees, housing, food, books, transportation, and personal expenses. Your actual cost depends on campus, lifestyle, aid, and residency.

Figures below reflect UC’s **2026–27** published estimates for new undergraduates (averages across the system). Always re-check [UC Tuition & cost of attendance](https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/tuition-financial-aid/tuition-cost-of-attendance/) before making money decisions.

---

## California residents (2026–27 estimates)

| | On campus | Off campus |
|--|-----------|------------|
| UC Tuition | $15,588 | $15,588 |
| Campus fees | ~$1,800 | ~$1,800 |
| Housing & meals (direct or indirect) | ~$21,200 campus | ~$19,500 off-campus estimate |
| Health insurance allowance/fee | ~$3,700 | ~$3,700 |
| Books & supplies | ~$1,400 | ~$1,400 |
| Transportation (est.) | ~$1,000–$4,500 range in UC tables | higher if commuting |
| **Total COA (UC published averages)** | **~$47,388** | **~$46,488** |

**Direct costs** (billed by the university) are much lower if you live off campus without a university housing contract - but then rent is your problem to budget.

---

## Nonresidents (2026–27 estimates)

Nonresident tuition is dramatically higher:

| | On campus | Off campus |
|--|-----------|------------|
| UC Tuition (includes nonresident supplemental tuition) | ~$54,858 | ~$54,858 |
| **Total COA (UC averages)** | **~$86,658** | **~$85,758** |

**Important:** Becoming a California resident for tuition as an undergraduate with nonresident parents is **very difficult**. Do not plan on “I’ll reclassify sophomore year” without reading [UC residency policy](http://ucop.edu/residency/index.html) carefully.

---

## Tuition Stability Plan

UC states that for many students, **tuition remains flat for up to six years** once you enter (Tuition Stability Plan). That helps multi-year planning, but **campus fees, housing, and food still move**.

---

## Financial aid reality check

UC reports that a large share of California undergraduates receive grants/scholarships (UC has cited averages around **~$22,000** in gift aid for those who receive it - verify current stats on UC’s site). Aid stacks from:

- Federal (FAFSA) 
- State (including California programs for eligible students) 
- UC grants and campus scholarships 
- Outside scholarships 

**Middle-income Bay Area families** often overestimate or underestimate net price. Run:

- [UC Net Cost Calculator](https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/tuition-financial-aid/) (via UC admissions financial aid pages) 
- Campus-specific calculators 
- FAFSA / California Dream Act application on time 

---

## Community college → UC and total cost

A major reason transfer paths matter financially:

| Path | Rough cost idea |
|------|-----------------|
| 4 years UC residential | 4 × full UC COA (minus aid) |
| 2 years CCC + 2 years UC | 2 × low CCC cost + 2 × UC COA |

California community college tuition/fees are a small fraction of UC tuition. Dual enrollment in high school can shift even more lower-division units into the low-cost phase.

This is not only about money - but for many families it is the difference between feasible and stressful.

---

## Other cost levers

- **Commuter vs residential** campuses/lifestyles 
- **Summer classes** at CCC vs UC summer session pricing 
- **AP/IB credits** that reduce terms needed (campus-specific) 
- **Time to degree** - graduating in 3–3.5 years after transfer can beat a long UC-only path 
- **Health insurance** waivers if you have comparable coverage
